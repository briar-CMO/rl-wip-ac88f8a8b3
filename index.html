Creative & Personas

Applying the ACA system · who we speak to, and where the gaps are · both markets

How we figure out personas & ACA gaps

  • 1. Define personas from evidence, not guesses — mine reviews, surveys, the converting search terms and the age/gender ROAS (the ACA Persona Protocol).
  • 2. Tag every live ad to Persona × Angle × Format. The library becomes a coverage map.
  • 3. Build the coverage matrix (below). Empty cells are the holes.
  • 4. Brief into the biggest holes first (brief → test → re-audit). That is the flywheel.

RevitaLash personas (first pass)

The Age-Aware 45–60
Lashes & brows thinned with age / menopause. Top-spending ROAS demo.
Driver: Look like myself again, not ‘done’.
What lands: Before/after result, awards, ‘physician-developed since 2006’.
The Young Maximalist 18–34
Wants longer, fuller, ‘fake-looking’ lashes without extensions. Highest ROAS by age.
Driver: Beauty & identity; TikTok-native.
What lands: UGC demo, ‘people think my lashes are fake’, mascara, trial kit.
The Hair-Thinning Woman 35+
Shedding / thinning hair (postpartum, stress, age). High ad engagement.
Driver: Anxiety about visible thinning.
What lands: ‘Hair Ugly’ pain+result, hairdresser approval, the new mask.
The Ingredient-Cautious 30–55
Worried about prostaglandins / side effects. Literally searching ‘prostaglandin-free’ (and converting).
Driver: Safety, no nasty surprises.
What lands: Sensitive formula, ingredient transparency, answer the objection.
The Brow-Grower 25–45
Over-plucked / microbladed-out brows wanting natural fullness.
Driver: Frame the face, low-maintenance.
What lands: RevitaBrow UGC, before/after, Allure best-brow proof.
The Gifter any
Buying for mum / partner. Seasonal.
Driver: Give something premium and thoughtful.
What lands: Bundles, gift-with-purchase, occasions.

The biggest gaps to brief next

  • The Ingredient-Cautious persona converts in search but has no creative. People search ‘prostaglandin-free’ and buy — meet it head-on with a safety / sensitive angle. (Evidence-backed.)
  • No competitor-comparison creative, yet grandelash, rapidlash and lilash convert in search. Conquest creative is a clear, evidence-backed gap.
  • Proof-at-scale and credibility angles are underused — ‘90% recommend’, physician-developed, ‘since 2006’. The heritage carries the brand story as trust, without targeting anyone in a vulnerable moment.
  • Gifter / seasonal is thin — a Q4 and occasions opportunity.
